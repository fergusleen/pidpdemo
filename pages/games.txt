Games On This System

The games directory is a small time capsule of classic Unix fun.
If you want a quick demonstration, these are the best bets.

Adventure
  The original great text adventure.  Slow down, read closely, and
  explore.  It remains one of the clearest examples of how powerful
  plain text and a good imagination can be.

Zork
  A richer commercial-era descendant of the adventure tradition, with
  sharper writing, a larger world, and a stronger sense of voice.

Rogue-style play
  `hack` is the standout here: dungeon exploration, monsters, loot,
  and permanent consequences.  It points toward an entire later genre.

Robots
  Fast, simple, and excellent for a short terminal demo.  It shows how
  little a game needs, if the underlying idea is good.

Trek
  A command-line Star Trek strategy game and a fine example of early
  computing culture meeting science fiction fandom.  It can also be
  launched directly from this menu for a quick demonstration.

Wump, chess, backgammon, boggle, hangman, banner, and quiz are also
present if you want something brief, familiar, or easy to explain.

Taken together, these games show that old Unix systems were never only
for work.  They were social machines, teaching machines, and playful
machines too.
